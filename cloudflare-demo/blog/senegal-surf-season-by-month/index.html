Senegal Surf Season by Month on Ngor Island

Who is this for?

Born and raised in Dakar, Kofi has been surfing Ngor's waves since age 10. Licensed by the Senegalese Federation of Surfing, he brings 15 years of coaching experience and intimate knowledge of every break around the island.

If you are planning a surf trip to Dakar, the first question is usually simple: when should you go? The honest answer is that the best senegal surf season on Ngor Island runs from November to April, but each month has its own rhythm in the water, on the reef, and around the island. Knowing those differences is what turns a good trip into one timed perfectly for your level, your expectations, and the kind of waves you actually want to surf.

Ngor Island sits just 400 metres off the Dakar coast, reached by a five-minute pirogue crossing from Ngor beach. Out here, the Atlantic swell meets volcanic reef in a way that creates two very different signature waves: Ngor Right, fast and hollow when the swell and tide line up, and Ngor Left, longer, more forgiving, and often the better option for surfers who want time on the face. At Ngor Surfcamp Teranga, we watch these patterns every week through the season, and this is the month-by-month guide we use when helping guests choose the right window.

Why timing matters on Ngor Island

A lot of surf articles make “season” sound like a switch that flips on and off. Senegal is more subtle than that. The prime months are consistent because the North Atlantic starts sending dependable swell, the wind patterns are cleaner, and the weather is pleasant without the tropical instability that can affect other times of year.

But consistency is not the same thing as uniformity. November often feels different from January. March can offer a very different trip from December, even if both are firmly in the main season. Some months favour surfers looking for shape and manageable size. Others are better for experienced surfers chasing speed on the reef. Crowd levels also shift, especially around holiday dates.

On Ngor, those differences matter because the two main waves react distinctly. Ngor Right is the headline wave for many visitors, and for good reason. It can be quick, hollow, and technical, especially when clean swell hits the reef at the right angle. Ngor Left, by contrast, tends to offer a softer entry point: a longer wall, more room to set up turns, and a friendlier platform for progression.

On Ngor Island, the best month is not the same for every surfer.

If you are still deciding where to stay, our surf camp setup on Ngor Island is designed exactly for this kind of seasonal planning: rooms for different budgets, breakfast and dinner included, surf guiding, theory sessions, a pool for recovery, and options like coaching, video analysis, board rental, and wetsuit rental when needed.

Understanding the ingredients: swell, wind, water, and crowds

Before going month by month, it helps to know what actually shapes surf quality here.

Swell windows

The prime season from November to April is driven by North Atlantic activity. Swells become more regular, and there are enough windows to build a trip around. You are not relying on one lucky pulse; instead, you are arriving during a period when the ocean is simply more dependable.

Ngor Right generally likes a solid pulse with enough period to wrap onto the reef and stand up properly. Too weak, and it can feel underwhelming. When the swell has some push, the wave shows its real character: quick takeoff, hollow pocket, little time to hesitate. Ngor Left can handle more variation and often gives surfers more options when the right is too sharp or too busy.

Wind patterns

Wind is the hidden hero of the senegal surf season. During the main months, mornings are often the cleanest. Light wind or favourable offshore flow can groom the reef beautifully, especially before midday. This is why early sessions matter here. You can still score later in the day, but dawn patrol regularly gives the most polished conditions.

Water temperature

The water on this stretch of the Atlantic is cooler than many first-time visitors expect. It is not icy, but neither is it tropical-bath warm through the entire season. Most surfers are comfortable in boardshorts on warmer days, though many prefer a spring suit or thin wetsuit for longer sessions, early mornings, or if they get cold easily. That is why we offer wetsuit rental at the camp.

Crowd levels

Dakar is no longer a secret. Ngor remains one of the best-known surf zones in Senegal, and during prime weeks the lineup can reflect that. The good news is that crowd level is not fixed; it changes through the season. Early season can feel more spacious. Holiday windows bring more traffic. Late season can be excellent if you know what you are targeting.

November: the season wakes up

November is one of the most underrated months to surf Ngor Island. The first real pulse of consistency starts showing, the weather settles into a more comfortable pattern, and there is often a freshness to the month that experienced surfers appreciate. The season is beginning, but it has not yet reached the busiest flow of visitors.

For many intermediate surfers, November is an excellent entry point into Senegal. You can get quality days without immediately dealing with the highest concentration of travelling surfers. Ngor Left often shines here, offering enough wall to work on positioning and line choice. Ngor Right can already turn on, especially when a proper swell arrives, but it often feels a little less pressured than during peak holiday weeks.

Wind in November is often favourable in the mornings, and the air temperature is comfortable rather than heavy. Water can feel cool enough that a spring suit is a smart option for some surfers, especially for first light sessions.

Who should choose November? Surfers looking for a strong balance: good chance of swell, cleaner lineups than peak periods, and enough variability to suit both progression and more performance-driven sessions.

December: reliable, clean, and one of the best all-round months

December is where many people begin to understand why Ngor has earned its reputation. Swell frequency improves, the weather is stable, and there are many mornings where the lineup looks almost composed by hand: long Atlantic lines, textured volcanic reef, and enough energy in the water for both Ngor Right and Ngor Left to offer distinct options.

If you want a month that works for a broad range of surfers, December is hard to beat. Intermediates can usually find approachable windows on the left and smaller days on the right, while advanced surfers still get enough quality to hunt for speed and tighter pockets when the swell fills in.

The one thing to note is crowds. December, especially around Christmas and New Year, can get busier. Not unmanageable, but busier. This is where local knowledge matters. At Ngor Surfcamp Teranga, our surf guiding is not just about driving you to a spot and pointing at the ocean. It is about timing tides, understanding how the reef is reacting, and helping guests choose the session that suits their level.

December is often the month when Ngor feels at once accessible and world-class.

December also suits travellers who want the full island experience. Beyond the surf, Ngor has a rhythm that is calm but never sleepy: fishermen, pirogues, rooftop views back toward Dakar, and that very specific evening light over the Atlantic. If you are curious about the setting itself, read more about life on Ngor Island.

January: prime season confidence

January is prime senegal surf season in full expression. The month often brings the kind of dependability people hope for when they book a surf trip abroad. Swell windows are regular, mornings can be beautifully clean, and the atmosphere on the island feels dialled into surf life.

For advanced surfers, January is one of the top choices to experience Ngor Right with some proper shape. When the reef starts drawing enough water, the wave becomes exactly what its reputation suggests: fast, hollow, and not forgiving of poor positioning. You need commitment on the takeoff, and once you are in, the line tends to reveal itself quickly.

For intermediates, January is still a good month, but it rewards honest self-assessment. There will be days when Ngor Left is the obvious call and days when the right is for stronger, more confident surfers only. This is one of the reasons we put time into theory sessions and optional video analysis at the camp. Reading a reef break from shore is one thing; understanding where it actually lets you in is another.

Crowds in January can remain noticeable, especially early in the month, but session timing makes a difference. Dawn patrol is often your friend. There are mornings here when the crossing is quiet, the lineup still half asleep, and the reef is at its best.

February: perhaps the sweet spot

If someone asks us for a blind recommendation without much more detail, February is often the month we mention first. Not because every day is perfect, but because the overall balance can be exceptional. There is still solid seasonal consistency, but the post-holiday atmosphere can feel a little more settled. The island breathes easier, and the lineups often do too.

Wave quality in February is frequently excellent. The North Atlantic continues to send swell, and the local wind patterns still favour clean sessions. Ngor Right can be superb on the right pulse, with enough speed and curve to keep experienced surfers very happy. Ngor Left remains a reliable option for longer rides and more room to build confidence.

For many intermediate-to-advanced surfers, February is the sweet spot between quality and manageability. You get the heart of the season without always inheriting the busiest travel dates. Water temperature remains similar to the core winter months: fresh enough that some surfers appreciate a bit of neoprene, especially if they are surfing multiple sessions a day.

From a coaching perspective, February is also productive. When conditions are consistent, progression accelerates. Guests staying at Ngor Surfcamp Teranga can use one session to test ideas, then revisit the reef with a clearer plan the next morning.

Key Takeaways
  • SUMMARY:
  • November starts the season with quality and lighter pressure
  • December and January bring classic prime-season consistency
  • February often offers the best balance of surf quality and crowd level

March: warm rhythm, steady opportunities

March is an interesting month on Ngor because it still sits firmly in the good season, yet the feel of the trip can become a touch more relaxed. There are still quality swells, the weather remains appealing, and many surfers find March especially enjoyable because it offers enough consistency without the sense of peak-season intensity.

By March, local conditions often feel familiar and readable if you have spent time on the island. Mornings continue to be the best bet for clean surf. The right still has real potential, though some days may feel slightly less powerful than the strongest winter pulses. The left keeps doing what makes it so valuable on Ngor: giving surfers a wave with time on the face and options for progression.

March is a very good month for surfers who want a trip that mixes wave quality with lifestyle. There is surf to score, but there is also space to enjoy the rest of the experience: breakfast after dawn patrol, theory sessions to make sense of what the ocean is doing, a recovery dip in the pool, dinner on the island as Dakar lights flicker across the water.

If your goal is to improve rather than simply chase the heaviest day possible, March deserves serious attention. It is often one of the most constructive months for building confidence on reef waves.

Did You Know?

FACT: Ngor Right and Ngor Left break on volcanic reef, but they offer very different rides: the right is quicker and hollower, while the left is usually longer and mellower.

April: the tail end of the prime window

April is still part of the recommended season, but it is also the month where you can start to feel the prime window tapering. That does not mean poor surf. It means more variability. You can absolutely score excellent sessions in April, especially if you stay long enough to let the good windows come to you. But compared with January or February, the consistency is usually less automatic.

This is a month for flexible expectations and smart timing. If a good swell arrives with favourable wind, Ngor can be beautiful. If the swell is weaker, the left often becomes the more useful option. For intermediate surfers, this can still be a rewarding month because there are often approachable days and a calmer overall feel to the trip.

Advanced surfers who come in April should know what they are chasing. You may not get the same run of classic winter pulses, but you can still find high-quality reef sessions when the charts align. This is where local guidance, tide awareness, and patience matter most.

By the end of April, you are approaching the shoulder of the season. From May to October, the island tends to be much flatter and less reliable, which is why we clearly advise travellers to focus on November through April for surf travel.

April can still deliver, but it rewards surfers who understand that quality comes in windows, not guarantees.

Which month is best for your level?

The short answer is that Ngor works for a range of surfers, but it especially suits intermediates to advanced surfers who want to learn a reef properly rather than just tick off a destination.

Best months for intermediates

December, February, and March are often the strongest choices. They usually offer enough consistent surf to improve, with plenty of sessions where Ngor Left becomes the ideal classroom. November is also an excellent option for surfers who want fewer crowds and a bit more breathing room.

Best months for advanced surfers

January and February usually top the list, with December close behind. These months give you the best chance of seeing Ngor Right with the speed, shape, and intensity that made it famous.

Best months for lighter crowds

November and parts of February or March often feel more comfortable than the busiest holiday period. That said, crowd patterns can shift week to week. If lower pressure matters to you, ask us directly through our booking page before finalising dates.

Best months for a first Senegal trip

If it is your first time surfing in Senegal, December through February is the safest recommendation. You are arriving in the most dependable part of the season, and the island is fully in its surf rhythm.

Action Checklist
  • CHECKLIST:
  • Choose December to February for the most dependable overall conditions
  • Choose November or March for a strong quality-to-crowd balance
  • Choose April only if you are flexible and understand the season is tapering

What to pack for the Ngor surf season

One mistake visitors make is packing for tropical beach surf instead of Atlantic reef surf. Dakar has sun and warmth, yes, but the ocean deserves respect.

Bring reef awareness before anything else. Good booties are not essential for everyone, but some surfers like them, especially if they are not used to volcanic reef entries and exits. A spring suit or thin wetsuit is worth considering if you feel the cold or plan to surf early and often. If you prefer to travel light, we have wetsuit rental available at €5 per day and board rental at €15 per day.

Wax for the expected temperature, sunscreen that can handle long sessions, and a basic surf-first-aid mindset all make sense. If you are serious about improving, bring an open schedule too. The best surf trips on Ngor happen when you are not rushing in and out after one session.

At the camp, we include breakfast and dinner, and guests can add lunch if they want full simplicity between sessions. Airport transfer is also available, which matters more than people think after a long travel day into Dakar.

Why staying on Ngor Island changes the experience

You can surf Dakar from the mainland, but staying on Ngor Island changes the relationship completely. You wake up closer to the waves, closer to the tide, and closer to the tempo of the place. Instead of commuting into surf mode, you are already there.

That matters during the senegal surf season because timing is everything. A clean morning window is easier to seize when your routine starts on the island. So is an evening check of the reef, or a quick post-session debrief over dinner. At Ngor Surfcamp Teranga, that is the core experience: surf guiding grounded in local knowledge, supported by coaching options, theory sessions, and the simple comfort of returning from the lineup to a place built for surfers.

We are also fully FSS licensed, which matters. It means the operation is recognised by the Fédération Sénégalaise de Surf and built around the local surf ecosystem, not outside it.

The honest verdict: when should you go?

If you want the simplest answer, go between December and February. That is the safest recommendation for most surfers and the most dependable core of the season.

If you want a little more nuance, choose November for a strong early-season trip with fewer people, February for the best all-round balance, March for quality with a more relaxed feel, and April only if you are comfortable gambling slightly more for good windows.

The beauty of Ngor is that it does not offer one single type of trip. It offers different versions of a surf season, all shaped by the same reef, the same island crossing, and the same Atlantic energy arriving in different moods.
